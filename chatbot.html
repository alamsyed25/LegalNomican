I need help understanding force majeure clauses in my contract.

2:11 PM

Force majeure clauses excuse a party from performance when extraordinary events prevent them from fulfilling their obligations under a contract.

These clauses typically cover events such as:

  • Natural disasters (earthquakes, floods, hurricanes)
  • War or terrorism
  • Government actions or regulations
  • Pandemics (as we've seen with COVID-19)
